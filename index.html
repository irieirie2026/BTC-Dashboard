Power Law Theory

Giovanni Santostasi · Bitcoin Power Law Theory (PLT)

The Bitcoin Power Law Theory models BTC as a scale-invariant system governed by Price ≈ A × (days since Genesis)n with A = 10−16.493 and n = 5.68 (Genesis Block: 3 Jan 2009). Santostasi argues Bitcoin behaves more like a growing city or physical network than a mean-reverting asset: adoption, hash rate, and price reinforce each other through feedback loops.

Key scaling relations in the PLT: addresses grow ~t³, price scales with addresses² (Metcalfe), hash rate scales with price², yielding a consolidated price ~t⁶ fair line. Bubbles punctuate the trend (Moore's law + halvings + security-driven FOMO) but historically revert toward the corridor — scale invariance implies ETF inflows extend the path rather than breaking it.

Research: The Bitcoin Power Law Theory (Medium) · bitcoinpower.law. Not financial advice — the curve can fail; use as a long-horizon framework only.
