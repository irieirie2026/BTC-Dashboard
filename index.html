Power Law Theory

Giovanni Santostasi · Bitcoin Power Law Theory (PLT)

The Bitcoin Power Law Theory models BTC as a scale-invariant system governed by Price ≈ A × (days since Genesis)n with A = 10−16.493 and n = 5.68 (Genesis Block: 3 Jan 2009). Santostasi argues Bitcoin behaves more like a growing city or physical network than a mean-reverting asset: adoption, hash rate, and price reinforce each other through feedback loops.

Key scaling relations in the PLT: addresses grow ~t³, price scales with addresses² (Metcalfe), hash rate scales with price², yielding a consolidated price ~t⁶ fair line. Bubbles punctuate the trend (Moore's law + halvings + security-driven FOMO) but historically revert toward the corridor — scale invariance implies ETF inflows extend the path rather than breaking it.

Research: The Bitcoin Power Law Theory (Medium) · bitcoinpower.law. Not financial advice — the curve can fail; use as a long-horizon framework only.

Global Liquidity Proxy

CB BS + Broad Money + FX Reserves

Global liquidity map

Click a country to sync charts

Geographic snapshot for the table year. Each country is colored by either total liquidity stock or its YoY change, depending on Map metric.

  • Proxy (USD): dark ocean base → bright teal = larger CB + money + reserves stock.
  • YoY %: muted green = growth; dark red = contraction (diverging dark-theme scale).
  • Hover for country name, value, proxy size, and data sources (WB · IMF · DB · Proxy).
  • Click any country to set View and reload all charts below for that economy.

Liquidity proxy · USD

Stacked components

Historical liquidity stock for the selected View. X-axis = calendar years (same scale as Regional chart).

  • Teal bars (left axis): three stacked components — central bank assets, broad money, FX reserves ex-gold.
  • Teal line (right axis): total proxy = sum of the three components in USD.
  • Bar segments use the 4-tier hierarchy; hover shows USD value and source badge per component.
  • Rising stacks = expanding official liquidity; shifts in color mix show whether CB, money, or reserves drove the change.

YoY growth & 3m SAR

Monthly SAR · dashed = projected through Dec 2026

Growth rates for the selected View — how fast the liquidity stock is changing, not its level.

  • Green filled (annual): YoY % from World Bank–frequency proxy — (stockₜ / stockₜ₋₁ − 1) × 100.
  • Left axis (green / cyan): YoY % — annual (filled green) and monthly (dashed cyan) on the World Bank / FRED proxy.
  • Right axis (gold): true 3m SAR from FRED — ((Lₜ / Lₜ₋₃)⁴ − 1) × 100 on US / Japan / Euro area feeds. Own scale so COVID QE spikes stay readable.
  • Both axes share a coincident 0% line — compare whether near-term SAR and YoY growth are above or below flat liquidity.
  • X-axis uses calendar years when only annual data exists; switches to a date axis (year ticks) when monthly FRED feeds are available.

BIS credit-to-GDP gap

Private NFC · BIS quarterly

Private credit cycle (BIS) — complements the liquidity stock charts above. Measures whether private non-financial credit is above or below its long-term trend.

  • Formula: credit-to-GDP ratio minus HP-filter trend, in percentage points of GDP.
  • Zero line: credit on trend; positive = above trend; negative = below trend.
  • +10 pp dashed red: BIS early-warning threshold for systemic credit risk.
  • −10 pp dashed cyan: deep below-trend reference.
  • X-axis = quarter-end dates with yearly ticks — aligned with the date scale used on Growth and Market overlay charts.
  • Hover shows the BIS quarter label (e.g. 2025-Q4) and gap value.

Regional aggregates

Fixed regional scopes

Cross-bloc comparison of liquidity stock levels (USD), fixed scopes — not affected by the View selector.

  • Each line = one regional aggregate (Global, Advanced, EM, East Asia, Europe, North America).
  • X-axis = calendar years, same linear year scale as the stacked proxy chart.
  • Use to judge relative size and divergence between blocs; steeper slopes = faster stock accumulation.

Market liquidity overlay

Yahoo Finance · delayed

Market-priced liquidity (delayed Yahoo) — optional cross-check vs slow official stock data.

  • TLT / HYG (left axis): long Treasuries and high-yield credit — rising prices often mean easier financial conditions.
  • VIX (right axis): equity volatility — spikes signal stress even when official liquidity stocks look stable.
  • X-axis = trade dates with month/year ticks, consistent with the Growth chart’s date mode.
  • Not included in the liquidity proxy formula — use for timing and risk appetite only.
